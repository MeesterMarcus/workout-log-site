What You Get

Where IronStack has the edge over Hevy

Hevy is a solid app — but these are the areas where IronStack goes further.

AI Coach After Every Workout

IronStack gives you personalized AI coaching insights after each completed session. Hevy's AI (HevyGPT) only generates new workouts — it doesn't coach you on sessions you've already done.

Two Distinct Rest Timers

Set one timer between sets and a different timer between exercises. Hevy uses a single rest timer. A small detail that makes a big difference for structured programs.

Same Monthly, Cheaper Yearly & Lifetime

Both apps are $2.99/mo, but IronStack is cheaper on every other plan: $19.99/yr vs Hevy's $23.99/yr, and $39.99 Lifetime vs Hevy's $74.99. If you're going long-term, IronStack saves you significantly.

No Signup Required

IronStack doesn't require an account. Download and start logging immediately. Hevy requires you to create an account before you can use the app.

English + Spanish

IronStack is fully localized in English and Spanish. Hevy is only available in English.

Truly Offline-First

IronStack works completely without internet — no account, no setup, no sync required. Hevy is cloud-first: you need an account and internet connection before you can start, and some features require connectivity to function.

Unlimited Custom Exercises

Create as many custom exercises as you want, free. Hevy caps you at 7 custom exercises on the free tier — you have to upgrade to Pro to add more.

Bottom Line

Should you switch from Hevy to IronStack?

Our verdict

Hevy is a capable app with AI workout generation, Apple Watch support, and no ads. If you're happy with Hevy, that's fair. But if you want post-workout AI coaching that analyzes sessions you've already completed, two separate rest timers, no account requirement, or better pricing — IronStack is the stronger choice. Both apps charge $2.99/mo, but IronStack is cheaper on yearly ($19.99 vs $23.99) and significantly cheaper on Lifetime ($39.99 vs Hevy's $74.99).

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is IronStack a good Hevy alternative?

Yes. Both apps cover the core logging experience well — workout notes, Apple Watch, plate calculator, PR tracking, folder organization, and offline support. The key differences: IronStack adds post-workout AI coaching (Hevy's AI only generates new workouts, it doesn't coach on completed sessions), offers two distinct rest timers instead of one, requires no account to start, charges less for Pro ($2.99/mo vs Hevy's ~$4.99/mo), and offers a $39.99 Lifetime plan that Hevy simply doesn't have.

How does IronStack's pricing compare to Hevy?

Both apps are free to start and cost $2.99/mo on the monthly plan. IronStack is cheaper on yearly ($19.99 vs Hevy's $23.99) and significantly cheaper on Lifetime ($39.99 vs Hevy's $74.99).

Do I need to create an account?

No. IronStack doesn't require you to sign up or create an account to start logging workouts. Just download and go.

Does IronStack work offline?

Yes. IronStack is built offline-first — no account or internet connection required at any point. Hevy can log sets offline once set up, but it requires an account and internet to get started, and is cloud-first by design, meaning your data lives on their servers.

Does IronStack have an Apple Watch app?

Yes. IronStack includes a native Apple Watch app for logging sets, reps, and weight directly from your wrist. Hevy does not currently offer an Apple Watch companion app.

Can I import my Hevy data into IronStack?

IronStack supports adding your exercises and workout templates manually. If you have a large history in Hevy, you can rebuild templates in IronStack and start fresh with all the new features going forward.
